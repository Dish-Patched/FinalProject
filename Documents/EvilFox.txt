Once clever and kind, the fox named Vex turned dark. Under the pale moonlight, he plotted atop the hill of shadows. With a flick of his tail, traps snapped shut, catching the unwary. All was silent after his deeds, as if the forest held its breath. He whispered to himself, "A secret only the wind knew," and vanished into the night. Time stood still when his eyes turned red, for that was the moment kindness died.
